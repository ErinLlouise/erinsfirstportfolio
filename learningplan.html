A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:


An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives

At EDA, I will manage myself and my workload by applying the following habits:
No zero days: I found what was mentioned in the course preperation to foundations was incredibly helpful which was ensuring practice every single day - no matter how big or small
Task responsibility and task balance: I have always strongly enforced task responsibility on myself especially when working with others. During this programme, the last thing I would want is to dissapoint anyone around me by not delivering what I promised. Achieving this would be by not only taking task responsibily, but being able to manage and balance my time when working with those tasks. I find implementing an agile project management/development system like scrum or kanban (depending on the project length/workload) works really well for me. I use backlog, to-do, doing, done, on my whiteboard to organise my tasks, and time myself using Toggl to ensure I am spending an appropropriate amount of time on each task. I really like the timeboxing strategy that EDA applies to sprint work, I find it really helpful when organising my time and tasks.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

At EDA, when things are buidling up and start getting to me, I will apply the following habits

Practicing the reccomended framework when approaching a difficult task. This includes:
Get intentional and habitual
Start your timer
Understand: Get clear on what is being asked
Plan: Plan your approach
Divide: Break into small steps
Make an attempt at solving it (write an initial solution)
Get unstuck
Improve your solution (Refactor)
Learn what you learned (Reflect)
Practice
I have found this framework works really well when I find a task difficult.

Practicing patience and taking care of my head:
As previously mentioned in my limitations, I tend to get easily impatient and frustrated if I find I am falling behind schedule or am struggling to understand something. I have recently started taking short breaks every 35mins or so where I do a few push ups, sit ups, stretch my whole body and walk around the house for a bit. I always feel 100% better, more relaxed and focussed when I do this and have found my workflow to be much more sufficient.
