What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

- Over foundations, I have found I have 'pubicly' portrayed myself as somewhat incapeable. Reason being I do not want to set an expectation to others and then fail to meet those promises. I think this mindset is a fixed mindset and it is holding me back from improving and progressing. It was not until the beginning of sprint five that I realised this and felt I needed to make some changes.

- Similar to the previously discussed, I have realised a deep rooted insecurity that I have had my whole life; intelligence insecurity. I have never felt academically strong and it was not until I started researching into growth mindset and nueroplasticity during sprint four that I started to think of myself differently. I think it took a while to understand that I am allowed to have this dream of becoming a web developer and I am allowed and perfectly able to progress overtime - so that is what I am doing.

- Lastly, I would like to mention some changes I made as a result of these realisations. Last night I sat down with some paper and pen and began discussing my feelings in regards to my progress over the last four and a half weeks. In retrospect, I am really proud of myself and I think I have done reasonably okay for someone with 0 JavaScript knowledge. But I do think I praised myself too easily, I could have put in more effort, therby increasing my motivation. This first point of somewhat unneccessary self expression may I think had triggered the habits that follow, especially during lockdown. It is not that I particularly lacked motivation to do my required work, I loved doing it. I think I sometimes lacked motivation to attempt to stop labelling myself as how I portrayed my intelligence to my peers. It is now that I finally understand what it means to have a growth mindset. I have been dwelling with this fixed mindset for pretty much my whole education without even realising it. Hence the following changes;

- A sleep-in is not a reward
- Plan my day better through self-awareness, "What can I do today to improve my programming skills?" or, "What am I currently not very strong at?" Setting small goals for myself if I feel I am behind and following through with them.
- My self reflection could be better. This was a realisation I had when journalling last night. I think if I continued to do this a few times a week, I will notice a difference in my motivation, workflow, and overall self-awareness (that I have previously claimed to be good at - guess there is always room for improvement).
- and some other personal changes that I won't mention.

In conclusion to this perhaps unneccessary essay, I do feel I have been a bit all over the place during foundations and lockdown certainly did not help. However, I am more excited than ever to continue on this learning journey with my fellow (very talented) peers. A promise that I have recently made to myself is to fully embrace the sometimes shitty and most-times wonderful learning curve by being fully honest with myself and my progress and projecting that honesty in how I portray myself. In other words, I am not an idiot ALL of the time





What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Crucial. Looking back on my first few weeks of foundations, these values can seem somewhat obvious; how else can one sustainably progress and succeed without these values? It was not until recently (re: my above rant) that these words actually sunk in.

Ones values are subjective, and funnily enough empathy and self-awareness have been mentioned a fair few times in these core blogs being some of my core values. I do have empathy and I do have self-awareness and I always have. What are their roles? Their roles are fully dependent on how I view and utilise them.

Erin five weeks ago would say that empathy is being able to put yourself in someone else's shoes. Erin now would say that empathy is being able to put yourself in someone elses shoes and being able to fully understand how you feel in your shoes, aka, self-awareness. It is all feeling and being able to understand, evaluate and reflect on your feelings and understand and respect other peoples feelings. By utilising these roles and understanding their purpose, encourages and supports ones learning journey at a sustainable and healthy accelaeration and velocity.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Two distinctive reasons come to mind. A) This academy's purpose is to help people be an employable web developer. A great requirement and value to those employers is to hire people with human skills; those who can work well alongside others, have good communications skills and motivation. B) As previously mentioned, being able to reflect and analyse and be honest with yourself and others enables you to maintain a sustainable and healthy web development career.
