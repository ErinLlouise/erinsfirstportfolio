Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to adapt to changes in an individual's environment by forming new neural connections over time. The brain changes continuouly throughout life in response to everything we do, and every experience we have. By understanding these principals, the benefits that neuroplasticity has on people include:

Recovery from brain events like strokes
Recovery from traumatic brain injuries
Ability to rewire functions in the brain (e.g., if an area that controls one sense is damaged, other areas may be able to pick up the slack)
Losing function in one area may enhance functions in other areas (e.g., if one sense is lost, the others may become heightened)
Enhanced memory abilities
Wide range of enhanced cognitive abilities
More effective learning.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

You can increase your neuroplasticity by doing the following:

Getting quality sleep - by getting 7-9 hours of sleep per nigh, allows the brain to reset brain connections. By doing so, your memory and learning capabilities increase therefore increasing neuroplasticity.
Learning - the brain benefits from learning the same way the body benefits from exersize. This can be from learning a new language, art style, instrument or any new skill as long as practice and memory are achieved while doing so.
Reduce stress - stress is a silent killer for neuroplasticity. If you struggle to reduce the sources of stress in your life, you must learn to change and control how you respond to it. This can include practicing gratitude, empathy and mindfulness or any other form of meditation.
Reading - studies show that reading fictional novels created heightened neuro connectivity in the brain. This stems from the power of thought and imagination. Imaginatio and action are integrated and engage the same neuro pathways. So by reading fictional novels and thus using your imagination, this strengthens and increases your neuroplasticity.





Discuss what growth mindset is and why it is relevant

People who adopt a growth mindset believe that their most basic abilities can be developed through dedication and hard-work. It is being able to use and understand the power of "yet". Having a growth mindset changes peoples views on accomplishing new tasks/skills as deep resilience and love of learning is gradually developed, utilised and essential.
