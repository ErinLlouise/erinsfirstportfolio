Emotional Qoutient
vs Intelligence Quotient

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is "the ability to percieve, control and evaluate emotion in onself and othere and to use that information appropriately"
- Normal E. Rosenthal, M.D

Psychologist, Daniel Goleman, describes EQ in his book, "Emotional Intelligence" (1995) as the ability to "handle ourself and relations".
This is shown through four domains as follows:
- self awareness - knowing what we feel and why we feel it
- self management - handling our distressing emotions efficiently
- Empathy - knowing what someone else is feeling
- Relationship management - putting all domains together for better socials skills and inducing a desired responses from others





How is it different to IQ?

IQ is defined by ones academic abilities and limitations, EQ is defined by ones ability to identify, control, and express emotions.





Why is it important?

EQ is important as it has been proven to help the development of childrens learning and social behaviour when practiced regularly in schools. When these four domains are practiced childrens participation, team work abilities etc are strengthed and as a result, their academic results.

EQ has also been proven in the advancement of individuals teamwork capabilites in the workplace when these four domains are regularly practiced, and therefore overall performance in their work.
